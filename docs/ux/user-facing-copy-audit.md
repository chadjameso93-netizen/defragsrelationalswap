# User-Facing Copy Audit

## Scope
Audited canonical routes and their primary components:
- `/` (Landing Home)
- `/login`
- `/onboarding`
- `/account`
- `/account/billing`
- `/world`
- `/dynamics` (formerly `/companion`)
- `/account/insights`

## Tone Adherence
The majority of the copy cleanly reflects the "calm, exact, minimal, premium, non-mystical, non-generic-SaaS" tone requested for Defrag 1.0. 
Phrases like "See where care, distance, and timing are clustering" and "Keep it small enough for a real insight" are excellent and grounded.

The migration from "Companion" to "Dynamics" successfully eliminated the most significant terminology drift.

## Findings & Conflicting Product Nouns

### "Insight Studio"
This noun appears heavily in `/account/insights` and `/account`. "Studio" is a generic SaaS term that conflicts with the established, clinical "Dynamics", "World", and "Insights" nouns.
**Action:** Strip "Studio" and refer to the feature and view simply as "Insights".

## Conclusion
The application's copy is highly consistent. Stripping "Studio" and ensuring "Insights" and "Dynamics" are the only interactive nouns resolves the remaining drift. There are no remaining conflicting product nouns or surfaces that feel like a different product.
